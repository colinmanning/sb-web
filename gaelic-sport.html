Gaelic Sports - An Introduction

There are 3 Gaelic sports, Gaelic Football, Hurling and Handball (not to be confused with Olympic Handball, Irish handball is a bit like squash played with your hand rather than a racquet).

Below you can experience all 3 sports via YouTube clips. Hope these previews whet your appetite for our sports, and if you want to have know more or have a go, join our club. Remember you can visit our Factbook page via the ling above, to keep up to date with latest developments.

Gaelic Football

Played by 2 teams, 15 players each side. A game has 2 halves, each 35 minutes. The game is played with a ball, which is pretty much the same as a soccer ball. Teams can score a goal by getting the ball in the net. A goal is worth 3 points. You can also score a single point by getting the ball over the goal post, but within upright posts. Goals and points are combined to determine the winner - so a score of 1-12 to 3-5 would actually be 15 points (1x3 + 13)  to 14 points (3x3 + 5).

The pitch is somewhat larger than a soccer pitch. Top games in Ireland attract crowds of up to 80,000.

Hurling/Camogie

Hurling (Camogie is the name given to the ladies version of the game) has the same team structure and scoring system as Gaelic Football, as described above. Hurling is a very ancient game, even mentioned in old Irish mythological stories. The game is played with a stick, called a hurley (or camán in Irhsh language). The small ball is known as a sliothar, and is slightly bigger than a tennis ball - harder than a tennis ball, but not as hard as a hockey ball. Unlike hockey, the ball is played both on the ground, but also extensively in the air, and players can catch the sliothar and pass it with their hands.

The game is very fast, and exciting to watch. The strongest Irish hurling team has been Kilkenny for many years, however this year Galway provided a string challenge, including defeating Kilkenny in the league, and forcing a reply in the All Ireland final, which Kilkenny subsequently won. Handball

Irish handball is a game where 2 players (although it can be played in a doubles format with 2 teams of 2) hit a small ball against a front and 2 side walls to score points. In terms of playing and scoring structure, it is similar to squash, and is often played in squash courts these days. However the original courts were outdoors, and called an "alley" - you can often see these alleys as you travel through towns and villages in rural Ireland.
The game was commonly played in school yards. The ball is larger and harder than a squash ball. There is a very strong tradition of playing handball in the USA.
